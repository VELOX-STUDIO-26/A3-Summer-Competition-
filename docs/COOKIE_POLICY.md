## What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit websites. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies help us:
- Keep you logged in
- Remember your preferences
- Understand how you use the Service
- Improve your experience

---

## Types of Cookies We Use

### 1. Essential Cookies

**Purpose**: Required for the Service to function
**Duration**: Session to 30 days
**Examples**:
- Authentication tokens (JWT)
- Session identifiers
- Security tokens
- CSRF protection tokens

These cookies cannot be disabled as they are necessary for basic functionality.

### 2. Preference Cookies

**Purpose**: Remember your settings and preferences
**Duration**: Up to 1 year
**Examples**:
- Language preferences
- Display settings (theme, font size)
- Notification preferences
- Learning path progress
- UI customizations

### 3. Analytics Cookies

**Purpose**: Help us understand usage patterns
**Duration**: Up to 2 years
**Examples**:
- Page view tracking
- Feature usage statistics
- Performance metrics
- Error reporting

Data collected is aggregated and anonymized where possible.

### 4. Functional Cookies

**Purpose**: Enable enhanced functionality
**Duration**: Up to 1 year
**Examples**:
- Chat history (encrypted)
- Recently accessed resources
- Bookmarked content
- Search history

## How to Manage Cookies

### Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through settings:

**Chrome**:
- Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies

**Firefox**:
- Preferences → Privacy & Security → Cookies

**Safari**:
- Preferences → Privacy → Cookies

**Edge**:
- Settings → Cookies and site permissions

### Your Choices

- **Accept All**: Full Service functionality
- **Essential Only**: Basic functionality only
- **Custom**: Select which categories to allow

Note: Disabling certain cookies may limit Service functionality.

## Cookie Duration

### Session Cookies
- Deleted when you close your browser
- Used for temporary data

### Persistent Cookies
- Remain for a set period (see table above)
- Used for preferences and analytics

### Expiration
- You can delete cookies manually
- Browser settings can auto-delete
- Some cookies have fixed expiration dates

## Local Storage and Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we use:

### LocalStorage
- Stores larger amounts of data client-side
- Used for: UI state, cached resources, offline data
- Duration: Until manually cleared

### SessionStorage
- Temporary storage for session data
- Used for: Form data, temporary preferences
- Duration: Until tab is closed

### IndexedDB
- Database for offline functionality
- Used for: Cached content, progress data
